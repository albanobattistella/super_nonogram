# Privacy policy - Super Nonogram

Super Nonogram is a puzzle game developed by Adil Hanney.

## First party data collection

Super Nonogram does not directly collect or share any sensitive or personal data, but the app uses third-party services that may collect data as described below.

All game data is stored locally on your device, including what level you're currently on and the generated puzzles.

## Third party data collection

### Google Ads

Updated versions of the app from v0.8.0 onwards do not include ads. No further data is shared with Google AdMob.

<details>
<summary>See ad practices from previous versions (v0.7.2 and earlier)</summary>

Super Nonogram used Google AdMob to display ads in older versions of the app.
AdMob may collect data about you, including your device's advertising ID, IP address, and location. You can read more about [how Google uses data from apps that use their services](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites).

When you first opened Super Nonogram, you were shown a consent prompt asking you to choose between personalized and non-personalized ads.
If you still use a version of Super Nonogram that contains ads, you can change your consent at any time by going to the settings menu in the game.

Note that ads were only used on the Android and iOS versions of Super Nonogram: other platforms (including the web) never contained ads.
</details>

### Pixabay API

Super Nonogram allows you to generate a puzzle based on a keyword. It does this by downloading an image from Pixabay using the Pixabay API. You can read [Pixabay's privacy policy](https://pixabay.com/service/privacy/) for more information.

Super Nonogram does not share any sensitive or personal information to Pixabay, unless you explicitly enter such information as part of your search query.
